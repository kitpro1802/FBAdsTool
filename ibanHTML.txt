The IBAN uniquely identifies an account held at a bank. An IBAN is not
replacing the national numbering systems and is also not a new bank
account number. It is a way of representing national account numbers in
an internationally recognised standard format. <br>

with a 2 character country code (ISO 3166), followed by a 2-digit
checksum and the Basic Bank Account Number (BBAN, ISO 13616). The BBAN
itself consists of a bank/branch code and the account number, both with
optional check digits.
